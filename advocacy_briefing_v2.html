03
The Three Products Video · Podcast · Website

Each medium does something the others can't. The video is shareable and emotionally direct. The podcast goes deeper, lets us walk through the argument. The website is where a legislator or journalist lands when they want to cite us — it hosts everything, adds context, and serves as our "united front."

i.
Digital · Medium 1 · Standard Product The Video (90s–2min)

Hits the "engage the public via media" impact criterion and travels well on social. HeyGen lets us generate a clean avatar-delivered script with B-roll overlays — far more polished than anything we could shoot ourselves in a weekend, and it keeps production from becoming the bottleneck.

HeyGen · avatar + VO Canva · B-roll + graphics Length · 90s–2min Format · MP4 · 16:9 + vertical cut
Proposed Angle

Open on a phone screen at 2am, algorithm feeding a teen more of what's hurting them. Cold voiceover: "The Surgeon General put a warning on this. California wrote the law. But the algorithm keeps scrolling." Build through stats (39.7% persistent sadness, 3+ hours = 2x depression risk, 95% of teens on social media). Land on: regulate design, not just content.

Script beat sheet · draft
00:00 — Hook: phone screen glow, 2am
00:10 — Problem: 39.7% of teens report persistent sadness
00:25 — Mechanism: infinite scroll, algorithmic amplification
00:45 — Policy gap: SB 976 + AB 56 only address content
01:10 — The ask: regulate design features, not posts
01:40 — CTA: "Tell CA legislators to close the design gap"
Input on the video
ii.
Audio · Medium 2 · Standard Product The Podcast / Audio Piece

A two-voice mini-podcast that uses conversation format to unpack the "design vs. content" distinction for listeners. ElevenLabs gives us two distinct, natural-sounding voices — one framing the problem, one carrying the policy argument. Canva for visual cover art + waveform treatment if we embed it in the website.

ElevenLabs · 2 voices Canva · cover art + waveform Length · 90s–2min Format · MP3
Proposed Angle

Two-host format. Voice A lays out the problem (CDC stats, teen testimonial framing). Voice B reframes: "We keep debating what kids are seeing. The real question is what the platform is doing to them." Walk through the cigarette-warning parallel. End with specific policy ask — build on SB 976, implement AB 56, add design audits.

Script beat sheet · draft
00:00 — Cold open: "Nearly 4 in 10 high schoolers..."
00:20 — Voice B reframe: design, not content
00:45 — Mechanism walk-through: infinite scroll, algorithmic loops
01:10 — What CA has done: SB 976, AB 56
01:30 — What's missing: design audits, exposure transparency
01:50 — CTA + sign-off
Input on the podcast
iii.
Digital · Medium 3 · Pending Approval The Campaign Website

A single-page advocacy site that becomes the public home for the campaign — hosts the video, embeds the podcast, frames the argument in readable sections, and offers a clear CTA (contact legislator, sign petition, share). The rubric maps cleanly: background, argument, evidence (5+ sources), medium-specific strengths (interactivity, shareability), strategic impact (hub for "united front"), presentation.

Framer / Carrd / Wix Custom domain optional Length · long-form scroll Sources · 5+ embedded
Proposed Structure

Hero: "Beyond Content Moderation." One-sentence thesis. Embedded video. Scroll into: the problem (stats), the mechanism (algorithm design), current CA policy, the gap, our three recommendations. Embedded podcast. Coalition of supporting orgs. CTA block. Citations footer.

Fallback plan if Dr. Chang says no: We swap this for a 750–1000 word op-ed pitched at the SF Chronicle or LA Times, using the same "design vs. content" framing. Minimal disruption — the argument, evidence, and CTA all transfer over. I'll have the op-ed draft ready by Sunday either way as insurance.
